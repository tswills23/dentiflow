# Emergency Dental Visit

**Category:** Emergency

## What It Is
An urgent, same-day or next-day appointment for patients experiencing dental pain, trauma, swelling, or other acute issues that need immediate attention. The visit focuses on diagnosing the problem, managing pain, and stabilizing the situation.

## Common Aliases
- Emergency appointment
- Urgent dental visit
- Same-day appointment
- Dental emergency
- Walk-in
- Pain visit
- Toothache appointment
- Urgent care dental

## Typical Duration
30-60 minutes for evaluation and initial treatment. Follow-up visits may be needed for definitive treatment.

## Insurance Note
Emergency exams are typically covered similarly to other diagnostic visits. Any follow-up treatment will depend on what's needed. Tell patients: "Emergency visits are covered by most dental plans. Let's focus on getting you in and comfortable — our team will go over any costs with you."

## Key Patient Questions

**Q: Can I come in today?**
A: "We'll do our best to get you in as soon as possible. Please call us at {{practice_phone}} so we can get you scheduled right away."

**Q: How much will it cost?**
A: "Let's focus on getting you in and feeling better. Our team will go over any costs with you before starting treatment."

**Q: What should I do in the meantime?**
A: Do NOT give medical advice. Say: "Please call us at {{practice_phone}} so we can give you guidance. If you're experiencing severe pain or swelling and we're closed, please go to your nearest emergency room."

**Q: Is this a real emergency?**
A: "If you're in pain or something doesn't feel right, it's worth getting checked out. Give us a call and we'll help you figure out the best next step."

## Booking Guidance
- NEVER send a booking link for emergencies
- ALWAYS direct the patient to call the practice phone number
- If after hours: advise ER for severe symptoms (pain, swelling, bleeding, trauma)
- If patient texts about emergency: Respond immediately with phone number and urgency
- Escalate to staff immediately per escalation rules

## Tone Guidance
Urgent, empathetic, and action-oriented. The patient is in distress. Acknowledge their pain immediately, skip the pleasantries, and get them to the fastest path to help. Do not ask unnecessary questions. Every message should move toward getting them seen.
