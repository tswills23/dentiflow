# Extraction (Tooth Removal)

**Category:** Oral Surgery

## What It Is
The removal of a tooth that is too damaged to repair, causing problems, or needs to come out for orthodontic or other reasons. Includes simple extractions (tooth is visible and accessible) and surgical extractions (tooth is impacted or broken below the gumline).

## Common Aliases
- Tooth pulling
- Tooth removal
- Having a tooth pulled
- Wisdom teeth removal
- Wisdom tooth extraction
- Surgical extraction

## Typical Duration
Simple extraction: 20-40 minutes. Surgical extraction: 45-90 minutes. Wisdom teeth (multiple): 60-120 minutes.

## Insurance Note
Simple extractions are often covered under "basic" at 70-80%. Surgical extractions and wisdom teeth may fall under "major" at 50%. Tell patients: "Extractions are covered by most dental plans. Our team will check your specific benefits and let you know what to expect cost-wise."

## Key Patient Questions

**Q: Does it hurt?**
A: "The area is fully numbed so you shouldn't feel pain during the procedure. The doctor will make sure you're comfortable before starting and will go over what to expect for recovery."

**Q: How long is recovery?**
A: "Recovery depends on the type of extraction. The doctor will give you detailed instructions, but most patients are feeling much better within a few days."

**Q: I need my wisdom teeth out — do you do that here?**
A: Check if practice offers surgical extractions. If yes: "Yes, we can help with that! Let's get you scheduled for an evaluation." If unsure: "Let's get you in for an evaluation and the doctor can go over the best plan for you."

**Q: What are my options to replace the tooth?**
A: "The doctor can go over replacement options like implants or bridges during your visit. They'll help you find the best solution."

## Booking Guidance
- If patient has been told they need an extraction: Book "Extraction" or "Oral Surgery" appointment
- If patient mentions wisdom teeth: Book an evaluation/consult first unless the practice prefers direct booking
- If patient is in pain: Treat as emergency, direct to call the office

## Tone Guidance
Empathetic and straightforward. Losing a tooth can be emotional. Be understanding without being overly clinical. If wisdom teeth, keep it casual — it's extremely common.
