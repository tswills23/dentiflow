# Comprehensive Exam

**Category:** Diagnostic / New Patient

## What It Is
A thorough first-visit examination that includes a full review of your teeth, gums, jaw, bite, and oral health history. Typically includes X-rays and may be paired with a cleaning. This is the standard starting point for new patients.

## Common Aliases
- New patient exam
- Full exam
- First visit
- Initial exam
- Comprehensive oral evaluation
- New patient appointment

## Typical Duration
60-90 minutes (includes X-rays and often a cleaning)

## Insurance Note
Comprehensive exams are typically covered once every 3-5 years depending on the plan. Tell patients: "New patient exams are usually covered by insurance — we'll verify your benefits so there are no surprises."

## Key Patient Questions

**Q: What happens at the first visit?**
A: "We'll take some X-rays, the doctor will do a thorough exam, and you'll likely get a cleaning too. It's a great way to get a full picture of your oral health."

**Q: Do I need to bring anything?**
A: "Just your insurance card and a photo ID. If you can fill out your new patient forms ahead of time, that saves time at the office."

**Q: How long does it take?**
A: "Plan for about an hour to an hour and a half for your first visit so we can be thorough and not feel rushed."

## Booking Guidance
- Appointment type: "New Patient Exam" or "Comprehensive Exam"
- This is the default recommendation for anyone who says they're a new patient or haven't been to this practice before
- If a new patient special exists in practice_config, mention it here

## Tone Guidance
Welcoming and reassuring. Many new patients are nervous — emphasize that the team is friendly, the process is easy, and they're making a great decision by coming in.
