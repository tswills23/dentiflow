# Periodontal Maintenance

**Category:** Preventive / Periodontal

## What It Is
A specialized cleaning for patients who have been treated for gum disease. It's more thorough than a standard cleaning and focuses on maintaining gum health to prevent the disease from progressing. Patients on perio maintenance typically come in every 3-4 months.

## Common Aliases
- Perio cleaning
- Deep cleaning follow-up
- Gum disease maintenance
- Periodontal recall
- 3-month cleaning
- Perio recall

## Typical Duration
60 minutes

## Insurance Note
Coverage varies more than standard cleanings. Some plans cover perio maintenance at a different rate or frequency than regular cleanings. Tell patients: "Coverage for periodontal maintenance varies by plan — our team will check your benefits and let you know what to expect before your visit."

## Key Patient Questions

**Q: Why do I need this instead of a regular cleaning?**
A: "That's a great question for your hygienist — they can walk you through exactly why this type of cleaning is recommended for you and what it involves."

**Q: How often do I need to come in?**
A: "Your care team will recommend the right schedule for you. Many patients on periodontal maintenance come in a bit more frequently to keep everything on track."

**Q: Is it more expensive than a regular cleaning?**
A: "Pricing depends on your specific needs and insurance coverage. Our team can go over everything with you before your appointment."

## Booking Guidance
- Appointment type: "Perio Maintenance" or "Periodontal Maintenance"
- Do NOT diagnose or tell a patient they need perio maintenance — only book it if they specifically ask for it or the practice has flagged them for it
- If a patient says they were told they need a deep cleaning, book them for a consult and let the office determine the right appointment type

## Tone Guidance
Supportive and encouraging. Patients on perio maintenance are actively managing their health — reinforce that they're doing the right thing by staying on schedule.
