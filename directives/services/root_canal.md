# Root Canal (Endodontic Treatment)

**Category:** Restorative / Endodontic

## What It Is
A procedure to save a tooth that is badly infected or damaged at the nerve. The dentist removes the infected tissue inside the tooth, cleans and seals it, and usually places a crown on top to protect it.

## Common Aliases
- Root canal therapy
- Root canal treatment
- Endodontic therapy
- RCT
- Nerve treatment

## Typical Duration
60-90 minutes per visit. May require 1-2 visits for the root canal itself, plus a follow-up visit for the crown.

## Insurance Note
Root canals are typically covered under the "major" tier at 50-80% depending on the plan. Tell patients: "Root canals are covered by most dental insurance plans. Our team will verify your benefits and walk you through costs before any treatment begins."

## Key Patient Questions

**Q: Does a root canal hurt?**
A: "Modern root canals are very different from their reputation. The area is completely numbed, and most patients say it feels similar to getting a filling. The goal is actually to relieve the pain you're already in."

**Q: How long is recovery?**
A: "Most patients feel back to normal within a day or two. The doctor will give you specific care instructions."

**Q: Do I really need a root canal?**
A: Do NOT confirm or deny. Say: "The doctor will go over everything with you, including all your options, so you can make the best decision for your situation. Let's get you in for an appointment."

**Q: Can I just pull the tooth instead?**
A: "That's a great conversation to have with the doctor — they'll walk you through all your options and help you decide what's best."

## Booking Guidance
- If patient has been told they need a root canal: Book "Root Canal" or "Endodontic" appointment
- If patient is in pain and hasn't been examined: Book emergency exam — do NOT assume root canal is needed
- If patient is nervous, emphasize comfort and modern techniques

## Tone Guidance
Calm, empathetic, and myth-busting. Many patients are terrified of root canals. Gently correct the misconception without being dismissive. Acknowledge their concern and emphasize that the procedure relieves pain rather than causing it.
