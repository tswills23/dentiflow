# Crown (Dental Crown)

**Category:** Restorative

## What It Is
A custom-made cap that covers the entire visible portion of a tooth to restore its shape, strength, and appearance. Crowns are used when a tooth is too damaged for a filling but can still be saved.

## Common Aliases
- Cap
- Dental cap
- Tooth crown
- Porcelain crown
- Ceramic crown
- Same-day crown
- CEREC crown

## Typical Duration
1-2 visits. Traditional crowns: first visit ~60-90 minutes (prep + temporary), second visit ~30-45 minutes (placement). Same-day crowns (if practice offers): single visit ~90-120 minutes.

## Insurance Note
Crowns are typically covered under the "major" tier at 50%, though coverage varies. Tell patients: "Crowns are covered by many insurance plans. Our team will check your benefits and give you a clear estimate before any work is done."

## Key Patient Questions

**Q: How many visits does it take?**
A: "That depends on the type of crown — some practices can do it in a single visit. Our team can let you know what to expect when you come in."

**Q: How long does a crown last?**
A: "The doctor can give you the best answer based on your situation. Crowns are designed to be a long-lasting solution."

**Q: I was told I need a crown — what's the next step?**
A: "Let's get you scheduled so the doctor can go over everything and get started. You'll know exactly what to expect before any work begins."

## Booking Guidance
- If patient has been told they need a crown: Book "Crown Prep" or "Restorative" appointment
- If patient is asking generally about crowns: Book an exam so the doctor can evaluate
- Never tell a patient they need a crown — only confirm and schedule if they've already been told

## Tone Guidance
Confident and reassuring. Crowns can feel like a big deal to patients. Emphasize that it's a common, well-established procedure and the result is a tooth that looks and feels natural.
