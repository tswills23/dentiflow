# Filling (Dental Restoration)

**Category:** Restorative

## What It Is
A procedure to repair a tooth that has been damaged by decay. The dentist removes the decayed portion and fills the area with a durable material to restore the tooth's shape and function.

## Common Aliases
- Cavity filling
- Tooth filling
- Dental restoration
- Composite filling
- White filling
- Silver filling
- Amalgam filling

## Typical Duration
30-60 minutes depending on the number and size of fillings

## Insurance Note
Fillings are generally covered under the "basic" tier of most dental plans, typically at 70-80%. Tell patients: "Fillings are covered by most dental insurance plans. Our team will verify your coverage and walk you through any out-of-pocket costs before we start."

## Key Patient Questions

**Q: Does getting a filling hurt?**
A: "The area is numbed before the procedure, so most patients feel comfortable throughout. If you're nervous, let us know — we'll make sure you're at ease."

**Q: How long does a filling last?**
A: "That's a great question for the doctor — they can go over what to expect based on your specific situation."

**Q: I think I have a cavity — what should I do?**
A: Do NOT confirm they have a cavity. Say: "Let's get you in for an exam so the doctor can take a look. If anything needs attention, they'll go over all your options with you."

## Booking Guidance
- If patient has already been told they need a filling: Book "Restorative" or "Filling" appointment
- If patient suspects a cavity but hasn't been examined: Book an exam first
- Never tell a patient they need a filling — only the doctor can determine that

## Tone Guidance
Matter-of-fact and reassuring. Fillings are one of the most common dental procedures — normalize it. If the patient is anxious, acknowledge it and emphasize the team's focus on comfort.
