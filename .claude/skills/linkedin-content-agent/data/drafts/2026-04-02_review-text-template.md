A 1-star Google review costs a dental practice $9,000 in lifetime value. Most practices find out about it 3 days after it posts.

By then the damage is done. The new patient who searched "dentist near me," saw the review, and called someone else is already scheduled somewhere else.

The fix is catching the unhappy patient before they post. Here's the exact workflow.

Text 1 fires 90 minutes post-visit:

"Hi [Name], thanks for coming in today. How was your experience? Reply with a number 1-5."

No link. No review ask. Just a question.

If they reply 4 or 5:

"Glad to hear it. Would you mind sharing that on Google? Here's the direct link: [URL]"

If they reply 1, 2, or 3:

"Thanks for letting us know. We want to make this right. [Name] from our team will reach out today."

That second branch is where most practices leave money on the table.

A patient who has a bad experience and gets zero follow-up will post the 1-star review. A patient who has the same experience and gets a personal callback from your office manager will often not post anything. Sometimes they come back.

The system also gives you real signal. Your Google reviews only show you patients who cared enough to post publicly. Your 1-3 replies show you the ones who quietly left.

Most practices are flying blind on patient sentiment. This fixes that with three texts and one branching rule.
