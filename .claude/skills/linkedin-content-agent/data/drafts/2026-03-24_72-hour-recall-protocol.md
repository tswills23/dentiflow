I stopped asking practices "do you do recall?"

Every practice does recall.

I started asking "show me the last 50 patients you contacted and what happened next."

That's when the room gets quiet.

After watching enough practices run recall into the ground, a pattern kept showing up. The problem is never intent. Every team I've talked to wants to follow up.

The problem is that human-led follow-up degrades under load. Every single time.

Here's what we built to fix it.

Rule 1: Patient status determines who speaks.

How long the patient has been overdue determines the voice used in outreach. Not the front desk's comfort level. Not whoever is least busy that day.

- 0 to 3 months overdue: Office coordinator voice
- 6 to 12 months overdue: Hygienist voice
- 12+ months overdue: Doctor voice

The longer they've been gone, the higher the authority needed to re-engage them. A front desk "just checking in" text doesn't move someone who hasn't been in for 14 months.

A message from their doctor does.

Rule 2: Time determines when messages go out.

- Day 0: Personal check-in. No CTA. Just reopen the loop.
- Day 1: Consequence frame. Soft CTA. "What does your schedule look like?"
- Day 3: Direct offer. Only goes to people who opened but didn't reply.

Day 0 has no booking ask on purpose. Patients who have been gone a long time need to feel safe before they commit.

Rule 3: Patient behavior determines what happens next.

- They say yes: Propose times and book.
- They say not now: Acknowledge it, exit the sequence, defer for later.
- They say nothing: Continue the timed sequence.

No pushing. No guilt. No "just following up again!" No switching the voice mid-sequence.

The reason this works is that it removes every decision from the staff. Who to contact, when to message, what to say, how to handle replies. All predetermined.

Staff don't have to think. THEY JUST HAVE TO NOT TURN IT OFF.

Most practices already have the patients. They already have the data. They just don't have a system that enforces follow-through without depending on someone remembering to do it between patients.

If your recall system is 'someone in the office remembers to call sometimes,' I have news for you and it's not good.

What does your current recall system actually look like on paper?
