"Our recall list is dead. Those patients are gone."

I hear this from practice owners constantly.

And almost every time, it's wrong.

Your recall list isn't cold. It's uncontacted.

There's a massive difference.

A cold list is strangers. They've never been to your practice, never met your team, never sat in your chair. Getting them to book requires trust-building from zero.

Your recall list is the opposite. These are people who already chose you. They drove to your office, filled out the paperwork, sat through the appointment, and paid you money.

They liked you enough to do all of that. At least once.

They didn't leave because they're "gone." They left because life got busy and nobody reminded them to come back.

"We tried recall and it didn't work" is usually code for "we sent one postcard in November and moved on with our lives."

The data backs this up.

Without a system: 5 to 8 percent reactivation.
With a structured, multi-touch sequence: 25 to 35 percent.

Out of 400 overdue patients, that's the difference between recovering 20 and recovering 140.

At $180 per hygiene visit, that's up to $19,440 in recovered production. Before any of them accept treatment while they're in the chair.

The list isn't dead. You just stopped talking to it.

This has been your regularly scheduled reminder that 'we should do something about recall' is not a strategy.

When was the last time you actually looked at your overdue patient count?
