I'm about to run a 500-patient SMS recall test.

Here's exactly how I designed it. And why.

500 patients. All 12+ months overdue. Split into two groups of 250.

One variable tested. Everything else held constant.

Group A gets a reason-based opener:
"I was going through some patient records and noticed it's been some time since we've seen you."

Group B gets an empathy-based opener:
"I know life gets busy and visits can slip. Just wanted to reach out and make sure you're doing okay."

Same doctor voice. Same Day 1 follow-up. Same Day 3 offer. Same reply handling.

The only difference is the first message.

What I keep seeing in practices is recall built entirely on gut feel. Someone on the team writes a message that "sounds good" and blasts it to the whole list.

No control group. No variable isolation. No way to know if it actually worked or if those patients would have come back anyway.

I wanted to answer one specific question:

Does giving the patient a reason for the outreach ("going through records") perform better than leading with empathy ("life gets busy")?

The winner becomes the control for every future test.

The sequence:

- Day 0: No booking CTA. Only job is to get a reply.
- Day 1: Soft ask about their schedule.
- Day 3: Offer. Only to patients who opened but didn't respond.

If they never opened, the offer won't help. If they already replied, they're in the booking flow.

I'll share everything when results come in. Reply rates, booking rates, opt-out rates, revenue recovered.

ALL OF IT.
