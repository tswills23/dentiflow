Every dental practice leaks revenue in the same 9 places. Most owners are only plugging 1 or 2 of them and wondering why the boat still sinks.

Here's the full map.

Leak 1: Demand Creation. You're spending $5K/month on ads but 78% of practices can't tell you which ads generate phone calls. You're buying traffic with no attribution.

Leak 2: Speed-to-Lead. A new patient calls. Nobody picks up. They call the next practice on Google. 38% of dental calls go unanswered. Average response time to inquiries: 47 hours.

Leak 3: Intake Control. The patient gets through, but they're booked into the wrong appointment type, wrong provider, or wrong time. Two hours later the schedule is a mess.

Leak 4: Smart Booking. High-production procedures scheduled during low-energy hours. No buffer time for same-day treatment. Priority slots unprotected.

Leak 5: Pre-Visit Preparation. One confirmation text 24 hours out. No-show rate sits at 15-20%. That's $50K-$100K/year in empty chairs.

Leak 6: Visit Experience. Doctor-owned. Good clinicians handle this. But what happens after the visit is where most practices fall apart.

Leak 7: Revenue Capture. Patient gets diagnosed with $4,000 in treatment. Says "let me think about it." Nobody follows up. The $4,000 evaporates.

Leak 8: Recall and Reactivation. 30-40% of patients never come back for their next hygiene visit. 70% won't return if you don't contact them within 6 months. $150K+ per year sitting untouched in your PMS.

Leak 9: Retention and Re-Entry. Completed visits with no pre-booked future care. Deferred treatment never revisited. Patients quietly decay out of the practice.

Most practices throw money at Leak 1 (ads) and maybe touch Leak 8 (recall postcards). The other 7 bleed silently.

Dental Economics found that stagnating practices follow a 13% revenue decline pattern. Not because of one catastrophic failure. Because of 7 small ones compounding.

The average practice loses 9% of adjusted production to uncollected revenue alone. Add no-shows, missed calls, unscheduled treatment, and lapsed recall, and you're looking at 20-30% of potential revenue walking out the door every year.

Running ads without fixing your follow-up is like pouring water into a bucket with no bottom. The water's not the problem. The bucket is the problem.

Which of these 9 leaks is costing your practice the most right now?
