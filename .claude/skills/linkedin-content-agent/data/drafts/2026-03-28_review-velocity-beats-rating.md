The dental practice with a 5.0 Google rating and 47 reviews is losing to the one with a 4.7 and 290.

Not might be losing. Is losing.

Google's local search algorithm doesn't care about your perfect score. It cares about volume and velocity. How many reviews you have. How fast they're coming in.

A practice getting 15-20 reviews a month will outrank the one with a flawless rating and 3 reviews from last quarter. Every time.

Most practice owners I talk to think their review strategy is fine. "We have a 4.9." Cool. So does every other practice within 5 miles. The tiebreaker is count.

And "we ask patients to leave reviews" is not a review strategy. That's hope with a clipboard.

Here's what actually happens. The front desk is slammed. They forget. When they do remember, the patient says "sure" and then never does it. No follow-up. No system. No tracking.

The timing is wrong too. The best moment to ask is 1-2 hours after the visit, when the patient is home, relaxed, and still remembers why they liked you. Not at checkout while they're pulling out their credit card.

72% of patients trust online reviews as much as a friend's recommendation. Which means your Google profile is your front door for every new patient who searches "dentist near me."

If your review strategy is "we have a sign at the front desk," I have questions about your other strategies too.

How many Google reviews did your practice get last month?
