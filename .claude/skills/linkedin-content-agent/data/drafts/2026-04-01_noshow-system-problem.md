Your no-show rate has nothing to do with your patients.

It has everything to do with what happens after they book.

Most practices send one confirmation text 24 hours before the appointment. One touchpoint across a 6-week gap. That's not a confirmation system. That's a coin flip.

Here's what's actually happening when a patient no-shows:

They forgot. They booked 6 weeks ago. Life moved on. The appointment didn't survive contact with a busy week.

They got anxious. The further from the appointment, the easier it is to avoid. Nobody wakes up excited about a dental visit. Without a nudge, avoidance wins every time.

Nobody followed up. The practice sent one text. The patient's inbox had 200 others that week. Yours lost.

Airlines send one gate reminder. You still show up. You know why?

Because if you don't, you lose $400 and you're sleeping in the terminal.

Dental appointments don't work like that. The patient doesn't lose anything today. The cavity they're skipping won't hurt until next year. The cleaning they missed won't show up as a bill this week. The cost of no-showing is invisible and deferred.

When the stakes feel low, one text isn't enough. That's not a character flaw. That's human nature.

The average no-show rate in dental is 15-20%. Practices that run multi-touch confirmation sequences (day of booking, 7 days out, 2 days out, morning of) cut that by 30-50%.

One study found SMS-based confirmation brought no-show rates down to 1.9%.

Not 19%. 1.9%.

The patients aren't broken. The follow-up cadence is.

This has been your daily reminder that your no-show rate is not a personality trait of your patients. It's a feature of your confirmation system.

What does your current pre-appointment sequence look like?
