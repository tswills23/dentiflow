5,493 calls came into a 3-location dental practice I sat down with this weekend. I pulled every single one.

61% went to voicemail.

Not after hours. Not on weekends. During business hours, between 8am and 5pm, 60% of calls hit voicemail. On Fridays it was 83%.

Of the calls that did get answered, only 16% ended with an appointment booked.

That means out of 5,493 inbound calls, roughly 833 turned into patients in chairs. The other 4,660 called, got nothing, and either called someone else or gave up.

I didn't find this in an industry report. I pulled it from CallRail. Real calls. Real transcripts. Their front desk.

396 of those calls were new patients trying to schedule. People who found the practice on Google, picked up the phone, and called. The front desk was too busy to answer. So they got a voicemail greeting and the practice got nothing.

Here's what killed me. Another 367 calls ended with "we'll call you back." That's 7% of all calls where the resolution was a promise. Not a booking. A promise.

Nobody was tracking any of this. Not the voicemail rate. Not the callback rate. Not the new patient calls that died on the vine. The Google Ads dashboard looked fine. The schedule had some gaps but nothing alarming.

Meanwhile 61% of demand was hitting a wall before a human even picked up the phone.

That's not a staffing problem. That's a systems problem dressed up as a busy day.

Somewhere right now a practice owner is refreshing their Google Ads dashboard while the phone rings to voicemail in the next room.
