A Harvard study found that responding within 60 seconds of an inbound inquiry increases close rates by 391%.

Here's what that looks like inside a dental practice.

Usually it looks like nothing. 38 percent of dental calls go unanswered. The patient calls, nobody picks up, they move on. No alarm fires. Nobody knows it happened.

Three scripts that fix the most common versions of this.

Script 1: Phone pickup.

"[Practice Name], this is [Name]. Thanks for calling. What can I help you with today?"

That's it. Not "How can I direct your call?" Not "Please hold." Not a voicemail that says you'll call back within one business day.

The only job is to get a live voice on the line before the patient finishes deciding if they're booking or Googling someone else.

Script 2: Web form response.

Patient fills out your new patient form at 9pm. Without automation, that's 10 hours of silence in a 60-second window.

Auto-text fires within 60 seconds:

"Hi [Name], this is [Practice Name]. Got your info -- someone from our team will follow up tomorrow morning to schedule your visit. Looking forward to meeting you."

That one text keeps the lead warm overnight.

Script 3: Missed call recovery.

Auto-text fires within 60 seconds of the missed call:

"Hi, this is [Practice Name]. Sorry we missed your call. We'll try you back shortly -- or feel free to reply here with a good time to reach you."

Then someone actually calls back. Today. Not tomorrow. Not when it's convenient.

The practices I've seen run this consistently don't spend more on marketing.

They just stopped letting inbound demand walk out the door.
