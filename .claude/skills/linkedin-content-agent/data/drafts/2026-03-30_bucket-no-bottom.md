Running Google Ads without fixing your follow-up is like pouring water into a bucket with no bottom.

The water isn't the problem. The bucket is the problem.

Practices I talk to spend $3K-$8K/month on marketing. Google Ads, SEO, mailers, maybe a social media agency.

That money generates leads. The leads call. And then....

62% of those calls go to voicemail. The ones that connect get a callback 6-10 hours later, if ever. Patients who do book no-show at 15-20%. The ones who show up get treatment diagnosed, say "let me think about it," and nobody follows up.

Every one of those steps is a leak. And nobody's measuring any of them.

Here's the math on a typical PPO practice:

- $5K/month in marketing spend
- 40-60 new patient inquiries generated
- 15-20 fall off before they ever book (slow follow-up, missed calls, no-shows)
- Another 10-15 have treatment diagnosed but unscheduled

That's $8K-$15K/month in revenue walking out the back door while you optimize the front.

The practice owner refreshes the Google Ads dashboard. Tweaks the headline. Bumps the budget. Meanwhile, HUNDREDS of overdue patients sit in their PMS untouched....

30% of diagnosed treatment goes unscheduled, and the no-show rate hasn't moved in a year.

That's not a marketing problem. That's 9 systems with 7 of them running on sticky notes and good intentions.

The cracks aren't bugs. At this point they're load-bearing.

Somewhere right now a practice owner is refreshing their Google Ads dashboard while the phone rings to voicemail in the next room.
