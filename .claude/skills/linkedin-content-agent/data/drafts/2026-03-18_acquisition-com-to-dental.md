Working for Alex Hormozi at Acquisition.com didn't just make me a better salesman.

It gave me a front row seat to how the best operators in the world diagnose and fix broken businesses.

I brought in eight figures of revenue in 10 months. But the real education wasn't the selling. It was what happened after.

I got to watch Hormozi's team tear apart business after business, across dozens of industries, and find the one constraint capping growth.

Gyms. Med spas. Home services. Coaching. SaaS. Hundreds of businesses.

After a while, you stop seeing individual businesses.

You start seeing patterns.

When I started looking at dental, the pattern was immediate.

Practices spending thousands on marketing with no system to answer the phones consistently....

Patients diagnosed with treatment that never gets scheduled....

Hundreds of thousands of dollars sitting in the PMS with no process for contacting the people they already paid to acquire.

The difference with dental is that the revenue sitting on the table is massive. And the systems to capture it barely exist.

Most practices are running on sticky notes and good intentions. That's like running a restaurant where nobody writes down reservations and the host just... remembers.

IT WORKS UNTIL IT DOESN'T.

That gap is why I started Dentiflow.ai. Not because dental needed more software. Because it needed the same operational thinking that was already working everywhere else.

I'm building this in public. Every framework, every test result, every system I build for dental practices, I'm sharing here. No pitch. No funnel. Just the playbooks that actually work.

Your overdue patient list isn't getting smaller while you think about it. It's just getting dustier.

What's one system in your practice that runs on memory instead of a process?
