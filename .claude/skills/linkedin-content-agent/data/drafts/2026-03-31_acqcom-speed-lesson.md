Three marketing agencies in 18 months. That was the track record of a practice owner I talked to last year.

Each time, the same story. "They promised us 30 new patients a month. We got leads but they never turned into patients."

At Acquisition.com, I watched this pattern play out across hundreds of businesses. And the problem was almost never the agency's competence. It was the model.

Here's how most dental marketing agencies work.

They charge $2K-$5K/month. They run your ads. They send you a report that says "you got 47 leads this month." And their job is done.

But 78% of dental practices can't tell you which of those 47 leads actually became patients. Nobody is connecting the ad click to the phone call to the booked appointment to the patient who showed up.

The agency gets paid whether those leads convert or not. Their incentive is lead volume, not patient revenue. So they optimize for clicks and form fills, not for patients in chairs.

This is the set-it-and-forget-it model. The agency sends monthly reports with impressive-looking numbers, and the practice owner has no way to verify what's actually working.

At Acq.com, we never let portfolio companies operate like this. If marketing spend couldn't be traced to closed revenue, the spend stopped until attribution was fixed. Not because the marketing was bad. Because unmeasured marketing is just hope with a budget.

The practice owner who fired 3 agencies didn't need a better agency. He needed a system that connected ad spend to booked patients. Without that connection, every agency looks like it's failing. Even the good ones.

Before you sign with another agency, ask one question: "How will I know which of your leads became patients?" If they can't answer that, you're buying hope.

What's your current process for tracking which marketing dollars actually produce patients?
