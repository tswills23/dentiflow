You don't have a marketing problem. You have a follow-up problem.

Most practices I talk to spend $5,000 to $15,000 a month on ads, SEO, mailers, and Google PPC.

Then a new patient calls and nobody picks up....

Or they pick up, book the exam, and the patient no-shows because nobody confirmed.

Or the patient comes in, gets diagnosed with a crown, and leaves without scheduling because the front desk was slammed.

That crown was worth $1,200. The implant case behind it was worth $4,000. The ortho consult they mentioned? Another $5,500.

None of it gets scheduled. Not because the patient said no. Because nobody asked.

That's like writing someone a check and then eating it. The money was right there. You had it in your hand.

The average practice has $250,000 or more in unscheduled treatment sitting in their PMS right now.

Crowns, implants, ortho, perio. Treatment that was diagnosed, documented, and never followed up on.

That's not a marketing problem. That's a hole in the bucket.

You're pouring new patients in the top and losing existing revenue out the bottom.

That's like ordering Door Dash with a full fridge. The food is right there, man.

OPEN THE DOOR!

Run this 60-second audit on your practice:

- How many patients from the last 90 days have diagnosed treatment that was never scheduled?

- How many hygiene patients are 6+ months overdue with no outreach sent?

- How many new patient calls went to voicemail last week?

If you don't know the answers, that's the problem.

The money isn't in getting more patients. It's in not losing the ones you already have.

This concludes today's episode of 'things your PMS has been screaming at you for 6 months.'

What's the biggest follow-up gap in your practice right now?
