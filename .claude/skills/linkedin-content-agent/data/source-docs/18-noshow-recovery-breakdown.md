# Source Doc 18: No-Show Recovery Breakdown

## The Problem

Average dental practice no-show rate: 15-20%. Some practices run 25-30%. Every no-show is a chair sitting empty that was supposed to generate $200-$500 in production.

A practice with 20 appointments/day and a 20% no-show rate loses 4 appointments per day. At $300 average production per visit, that's $1,200/day, $6,000/week, $312,000/year in lost revenue.

Most practices treat no-shows as a patient problem. It's a system problem.

## Why Patients No-Show

1. They forgot. Life happened.
2. They're anxious about the visit and the further away it gets, the easier it is to skip.
3. They got a better offer (another dentist responded faster).
4. No one confirmed with them. They assumed the practice would call if they needed to.
5. The gap between booking and visit was too long (3-6 weeks).

## Why Current Confirmation Systems Fail

- Most practices send one confirmation text 24-48 hours before the appointment.
- One touchpoint is not enough. The patient needs multiple touches over the gap period.
- Timing matters. A confirmation at 48 hours catches some. But one at 7 days and 2 days catches more.
- No escalation path. If the patient doesn't confirm, what happens? Usually nothing.

## The No-Show Recovery Sequence

### Pre-Visit Confirmation Cascade:
1. **Day of booking:** Confirmation text with appointment details and "add to calendar" link.
2. **7 days before:** Friendly reminder text. "Looking forward to seeing you on [date] at [time]. Reply Y to confirm."
3. **2 days before:** "Just confirming your appointment on [date] at [time]. Reply Y to confirm or call us to reschedule."
4. **Day of, morning:** "See you today at [time]! If anything changed, let us know."

### No-Confirm Escalation:
- If no confirmation 24 hours before: staff call or text escalation.
- If still no confirmation: waitlist patient gets offered the slot.
- Slot stays productive either way.

### Post No-Show Recovery:
1. **Same day (1 hour after missed appointment):** "We missed you today! No worries. Want to reschedule? Reply with a day/time that works."
2. **Next day:** "Just wanted to follow up. We have openings this week if you'd like to reschedule."
3. **Day 3:** If no response, move to recall sequence. They've effectively become overdue.

## Key Metrics

- Practices that implement multi-touch confirmation sequences reduce no-shows by 30-50%.
- The ROI is immediate: recovering 2 of 4 daily no-shows at $300/visit = $600/day = $156,000/year.
- The cost of the system is near zero compared to the revenue recovery.

## The System Connection

No-shows are connected to speed-to-lead. A patient who books after a fast response (under 60 seconds) is more engaged and less likely to no-show than one who booked after a 24-hour callback.

No-shows feed directly into recall. An unrecovered no-show becomes an overdue patient within 30-60 days. If you fix no-shows upstream, your recall list grows slower.

## Content Angles

- Problem awareness: the math of no-show cost ($312K/year for a typical practice).
- Framework: the 4-touch confirmation cascade.
- Contrarian: "Your no-show rate isn't a patient problem. It's a confirmation and follow-up problem."
- Actionable: the confirmation text templates (all 4 touches, copy-paste ready).
- Myth busting: "Patients who no-show don't care about their teeth." (Most forgot or got anxious.)
- System thinking: how no-shows connect to speed-to-lead upstream and recall downstream.
