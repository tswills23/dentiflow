# SPEED-TO-LEAD -- Complete Breakdown

## 1. Core Actions

### A. Immediate Lead Detection

**What must happen:**
- Detect every inbound phone call, web form, and online booking attempt.

**Why this is required:**
Inbound demand exists in a short buying window. If the system does not register the lead instantly, nothing downstream triggers and accountability is lost.

**What breaks if missing:**
- Voicemails pile up
- Web forms sit unseen
- Staff assumes someone else handled it
- Revenue loss with no alarm

### B. Sub-60-Second First Response Attempt

Harvard did a study on speed to first contact. They found that if you can respond to an inbound lead within 60 secs of them opting in, close rates increased 391%. THIS IS FREE MONEY

Hormozi story - https://www.youtube.com/shorts/2oSh0nDmBIM

**What must happen:**
Within 60 seconds one of the following occurs:
- Live answer
- Callback attempt
- Automated text acknowledgment

**Why this is required:**
Attention decays rapidly. The first minute is when urgency, emotion, and intent are highest.

**What breaks if missing:**
- Patients book elsewhere
- Staff blames lead quality
- You become a follow-up instead of the responder

### C. Multiple Same-Day Contact Attempts

**What must happen:**
- Call attempt #1
- Call attempt #2
- Text follow-up
- Optional email
(All same day)

**Why this is required:**
One missed call usually means timing mismatch, not lack of interest.

**What breaks if missing:**
- Leads marked unresponsive too early
- Paid demand wasted

### D. Clear Next Action

**What must happen:**
Every interaction ends with:
- Appointment booked OR
- Scheduled follow-up with owner + time

**Why this is required:**
Ambiguity kills ownership and momentum.

**What breaks if missing:**
- Leads fall into limbo
- No accountability

---

## 2. Ownership (Human vs System)

**Humans close. Systems chase.**

**System-owned:**
- Lead detection
- Retry logic
- Follow-up assignment

**Human-owned:**
- Booking

**Why this split is required:**
Humans are inconsistent under load. Systems are boring but reliable.

---

## 3. Failure Modes

- Missed Calls: Patients interpret this as disorganization and move on.
- Slow Callbacks: Urgency evaporates.
- Single Attempt Thinking: Timing mismatch is mistaken for disinterest.
- No State Tracking: Blind operations do not scale.

---

## 4. Recovery Logic

**Every failure must trigger recovery.**

- Missed call → Auto-text + callback queue
- No answer → Timed retry sequence
- After hours → Next-morning priority callback
- Front desk overloaded → Overflow handling

**Why required:**
Elite systems assume failure and design for it.

---

## 5. Outputs

Speed-to-Lead must output clean states only:
- Booked appointment
- Scheduled follow-up
- Marked unresponsive after X attempts

**Why this matters:**
Downstream systems cannot operate on ambiguity.
