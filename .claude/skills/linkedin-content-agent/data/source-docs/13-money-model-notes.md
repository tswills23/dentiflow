## CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

All the costs required to sell a new customer. Ad spend, salaries, commission for salesmen, software used etc. START TRACKING IF WE AREN'T ALREADY

### How to improve:

1. Better offer = higher response rates
2. Better conversion efficiency = more people take next steps
3. Cheaper media = lower CPMs
4. Cheaper Labor / more efficient labor = AI could help here
5. Cheaper software (if possible)

1-3 is where to focus on first and most often. Higher leverage. 4 & 5 are more "nice to haves"

Free offer take rate is 9x more than paid.

If they don't want free it's because:

1. Don't want it -- change what you're giving away
2. **Don't believe you**
3. Wrong people see it -- targeting

### Pros of free:

1. Will get more leads
2. Lower cost leads

### Cons of free:

   - Add friction (qualifications, types of questions, steps, targeting, etc)
2. No intention of buying
   - MYTH

## Discounts

Can be used instead of free if it costs the business a lot if the person misses the appointment (e.g. Lawyers, Dentists, Docs etc).

Could be better to use discounts for dental biz. Test both Free and Discount offers.

Don't use moderate discounts. Go BIG. 50% off vs 10 or 20% off will get more people to take the next step.

### 4 Ways to Display Discounts:

1. **Percentage off** -- "New client special, 87% off first visit"
2. **Absolute Amounts off** -- "$181 off first week (Normally $210)"
3. **Relative Equivalent off** -- "Save a Steak Dinner" (Negative perspective) / "Less than going out to Lunch" (Positive perspective)
4. **Simply the Discounted price next to Retail price** -- "$29 new client special"

### Pros of Discounts:

1. Compliant cheap leads (no fear of bait and switch stuff)
2. You actually collect some money
3. People come in expecting to spend money (mostly due to salesperson conviction)
4. 2 step sales process -- can get card on file and feels smooth ("its just part of our process" or "its just to complete your profile")
5. Upsells become WAY smoother

### Cons of Discounts:

1. Giving away the farm -- don't give away core offers or stuff that has hard cost unless advanced. We need to splinter our thing (100M offers). Must be a well understood thing or wont work.
2. Bargain Hoppers -- people complain about this, but really, they are already customers. Easier to upsell than someone cold.

## Decoy Offers

Advertise something free or discounted. Then when leads ask to learn more, you ALSO present a more valuable premium offer. The premium offer provides more features, benefits, bonuses, guarantees etc.

By putting your decoy offer and premium offer side by side, leads can see how much more valuable your premium offer is. Get more customers in total.

They either:
- Take the premium offer. GREAT
- If they take the decoy, also great. It gives you time to upgrade them rather than losing them completely

Either way you can close everyone. This makes it cheap and profitable to get new customers.

### How to make a decoy offer:

1. Advertise a lesser, smaller, or simpler version of your premium offer as a DECOY
2. When leads engage, offer both options, but emphasize the premium one. Use bonuses, premium value, and guarantees.

Key points:
- Make the contrast HUGE. The bigger the contrast, the better the deal, the more customers will take it
- Discount offers have higher show up rates than free offers
- If possible, present the premium offer first. If they ask for it, tell them about the decoy offer as well. Think about this like price anchoring.
- Get them to give you permission to sell them. Ask a "sorting/qual" question: "Are you here for free stuff OR lasting results?"
- When they say results (most people do), skip right to the premium offer
- If they say "free stuff", present the decoy then immediately contrast with the premium (price anchoring)
- After presenting both ask: "which do you think will get you to your goal faster?" or "which would you prefer?"
- **THIS IS AN ASSUMED SALE** -- Whoever is selling should act like this is a no brainer. "Most people who get the best results go with PREMIUM OFFER. I don't even know why we offer this other option"
- **When making your premium offer, Get EXCITED about it!** Your excitement motivates people to take the options that will give them the most value. Talk to the lead as if you already know they will accept your offer. ASSUMED CLOSE. "This is what everyone does. This is just a formality. Let me get your ID and CC so we can get you going." No Hype. Just friendly disposition. **Almost bored over how regularly people buy.**
- Surprise benefit (optional): If someone chooses the decoy offer, surprise them with a few low/zero cost features from the premium offer. Builds trust and good will fast. "Hey I'm gonna throw this in, even though its part of our premium offer just because I want you to get good results."

## Buy X Get Y Free

When customers buy something, they get other stuff free. The more free stuff they get, and the higher its value, the better it works.

Free offers get way more attention than discount offers. But if you only have one thing to sell, and you give it away, you go hungry. By selling more than one thing at once, you can turn discount offers into even stronger free offers.

**Great grand opening/reopening strategy** -- we can use this when we buy out a location to spin up cash fast. Use that cash to fund acquisitions of new locations.

Key points:
- Make this offer to existing customers for fast cash
- Even if customers prepay, you can still upsell them different stuff. People who "prepay" are self selected hyper buyers -- more likely to buy more from you
- **If customers only buy once, make the buy as big as you can**

## Pay Less Now or Pay More Later

You give people a choice to pay full price later OR pay a discounted price NOW. This removes all risk from the customer -- they pay later and only if they like it.

It combines the benefits of a delayed payment and a satisfaction guarantee. Anyone can sell this. Almost anyone will agree to pay later if they are satisfied. But, once they agree to pay later, you can get them to pay NOW with hefty discounts and valuable bonuses.

## Upsell Offers

With an attraction offer in place, you've got customers and cash. Now we want to maximize 30 day profits. To do that we make upsell offers AKA whatever we offer next.

When an offer solves a problem, another problem appears. The upsell is the solution to that next problem. Often, upsells make the majority of the profit, EVEN IF YOU SELL LESS OF THEM. They make or break a money model.

### Why Upsells fail OR feel slimy:

1. You offer something they don't want (too different or doesn't solve their problem)
2. You offer it at the wrong time (before they've experienced the problem)
3. You offer it the wrong way (they don't believe you)
4. Combination of the above

### What to sell? MORE, BETTER, NEW

1. More of what they just got (quantity) -- "Why have 1 burger when you can have 2?"
2. Better versions of it (quality)
3. New or complementary stuff (different) -- "Do you want fries and a coke with that burger?"

### Types of Upsells:

1. Classic
2. The Menu Upsell
3. Anchor
4. Rollover Upsell -- could be huge for dental biz

---

### Classic Upsell

Structure: "You can't have X without Y"

Provide free service that you can monetize another way. Works for one time services or even recurring (provided the recurring stream has high enough margins to pay for both).

Examples:
- **Lemonade Stand:** OFFER: Free lemonade. Upsell 1: lemonade ice cubes. Upsell 2: stainless steel "to go" cup + 20% off refills + $1 donation
- **Storage unit:** OFFER: Free month. Upsell 1: lock. Upsell 2: insurance
- **Weight loss:** OFFER: Free 28 day program. Upsell 1: $400 supplement package at nutrition orientation
- **Physical Therapist:** OFFER: 4 free treatments. Upsell 1: orthotics, bands, braces, oil etc

Important Points:
- Make sure the free thing doesn't cost you a lot (or anything at all)
- Make the upsell the next natural thing they would need to buy
- If people don't take the upsell after the free thing -- they're very unlikely to stay. Emphasize the importance. Think "this goes with this" rather than "want this?"
- If you suck at sales this is GOLD. If close rates are lower than 80% or more, you are doing it wrong

---

### Menu Upsell

1. **UNSELL** what they DON'T need
2. **Prescribe** what they DO need
3. Ask if they **PREFER A or B**
4. Make it **EASY to pay**

**Unselling** -- you unsell by telling customers what they don't need so you can emphasize what they do need. Instead of asking if they want to buy or not, you explain what they don't need as a way to get them excited about what they do need.

**Prescription Upsell** -- tell them what they DO need. Works well when offering a choice is inconvenient and you only have 1 thing to solve the problem. Two components: (1) explain how it integrates with offers they already bought, (2) personalize and detail how to maximize its value. Instead of asking if they want to buy, you explain how to use it as if they already have.

**A/B close** -- ask their preferences. Instead of asking if customers want to buy (yes or no), we ask which product they prefer. A or B. Either choice results in an upsell.

**Card on File close** -- "Do you want to use the card on file?" Not if they want to pay, refer to ways they already have.

Example: Massage Therapist -- Unsell (lymphatic massage), Prescribe (heat + trigger points + dynamic stretches), A/B (before work or on the way home?), Card on file.

---

### Anchor Upsell

Offer premium items first. If the customer is shocked by the price, offer a cheaper alternative, changing secondary features.

Presenting a premium version at 5-10x the price makes the main offer look like a better deal. Works best when the lower priced offer has the same core functions as the premium one.

Steps:
1. Present Anchor (really expensive thing)
2. Get the "gasp" (customer freaks out about cost)
3. Come to the rescue (ask if they care about what makes it premium)
4. Present the main offer (customer feels relieved at cheaper price)
5. Ask how they want to pay ("which card do you prefer: visa or amex?")

Important: If you treat the anchor like a fake, so will the customer. GO ALL IN. Make a premium offer you actually want someone to buy. CAPITALIZE ON THE WHALES.

---

### Rollover Upsell

Credit some or all of a customer's previous purchases toward your next offer. This gets WAY more people to buy.

**The Who:** Re-engage customers who left, rescue upset customers instead of refund, rescue other people's customers, upsell regular customers

**The What:** Upsell more of what they bought, something better, or something new. Roll their credit over to something more expensive. (e.g. teeth cleaning rolls into crown service)

**The How:** Apply all or part of the discount up front or spread it over time

Dentist Example: Upset customer paid $200 for cleaning, didn't think teeth got whiter. Upsell into whitening package (multiple sessions, at home kit, multiple deep cleanings). Credit the $200 toward the package.

## Downsells

Downsells begin after a customer says no. Find a solution to meet the customer's budget.

Two ways to downsell:
- **How they pay:** how much they pay now vs how much they pay over time
- **What they get:** change quantity, quality, or offer something different

**What not to do:** When someone says no, don't just offer the SAME THING at a lower price. You lose trust with that customer AND everyone else they tell.

### Payment Plans

- Work for any price tag
- People reject offers when they cost too much today, not necessarily too much overall
- Payment plans get the most buyers to pay the highest price by getting them to pay less in the moment but full price (or higher) over time

**7 steps of a payment plan downsell:**

1. Reward for paying in full rather than punish for paying over time. Increase price for payment plan. Present full price payment plan first, then ask if they want to save X% by prepaying instead.
2. Offer 3rd party financing, credit card, layaway options
3. Offer half now, half later
4. Check to see if they still want the thing. Temp check: "On a scale of 1-10 how bad do you wanna do this?" 8+ keep going. Below 8 ask "why not a 10?" then pivot to feature downselling.
5. Offer to split payments into 3
6. Offer evenly spread payments over the rest of the service
7. Offer a free trial

Payment plans have built in upsells. Make periodic offers for the original paid in full discount during their payment plan. Align payment schedule with paycheck dates.
