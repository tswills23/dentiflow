# 72-Hour Recall Recovery Playbook

## Revenue Recovery & Recall Protocol

This playbook is designed to help dental practices recover lost hygiene revenue by enforcing a consistent, psychology-aligned recall process.

Recall is not a reminder problem. It is a follow-up enforcement problem.

The goal is simple: re-engage overdue patients quickly, make responding effortless, and remove staff dependency.

---

## How the Recall Logic Works (Read This First)

This recall system is governed by three simple rules. When these rules are followed, recall becomes predictable and scalable. When they are broken, results degrade quickly.

**Rule 1: Patient status determines who speaks.**

The voice used in recall outreach is determined only by how long the patient has been overdue:

- 0-3 months overdue → Office / Care Coordinator voice
- 6-12 months overdue → Hygienist voice
- 12+ months overdue → Doctor or last provider voice

Once a voice is assigned, it does not change during the sequence.

**Rule 2: Time determines when messages go out, not who sends them.**

The 72-hour protocol controls timing only:

- Day 0 → Initial outreach
- Day 1 → Reinforcement follow-up
- Day 3 → Clarity and loop closure

Day number never changes the sender or voice.

**Rule 3: Patient behavior determines what happens next.**

- Positive response → propose times and book
- Not now / later → defer respectfully
- No response → continue the timed sequence

Patient behavior never triggers a voice change mid-sequence.

---

## Why This Fails When Humans Try to Run It

Most recall efforts break down not because the team doesn't care -- but because humans are biologically bad at consistency.

This protocol is intentionally designed to fail quickly when it's run manually -- so you can see where staff-led follow-up always degrades:

- **Response timing slips** -- replies aren't handled within 60-120 seconds, so the patient's intent decays and they ghost.
- **Voice inconsistency** -- staff flip between tones, templates, and senders based on comfort level instead of patient status.
- **Manual batching** -- messages get sent in bulk, not when the patient is most responsive.
- **Emotional override** -- staff hesitate to send messages from the doctor or hygienist.
- **Sequence corruption** -- the wrong follow-up goes out, or none goes out at all.

This is not a training issue. It's an enforcement issue. And unless every rule is followed exactly -- every time, for every patient -- results collapse.

## Day 0 -- Immediate Outreach (SMS + Email)

Purpose: Re-open the loop immediately using the correct voice based on how long the patient has been overdue.

### 0-3 Months Overdue -- Office Voice

**Template 1 -- SMS**

> Hi [Name], this is Sarah from [Practice Name].
> We haven't seen you for your regular visit and just wanted to check in.
> Would this week or next week work better to get you back on the schedule?

### 6-12 Months Overdue -- Hygienist Voice

**Template 1 -- SMS**

> Hi [Name], this is [Hygienist Name] from [Practice Name].
> Even when everything feels fine, regular visits help keep things on track.
> Would mornings or afternoons work better to come in?

### 12+ Months Overdue -- Doctor / Last Provider Voice

**Template 1 -- SMS**

> Hi [Name], this is Dr. [Last Name] from [Practice Name].
> I noticed it's been a while since I last saw you and wanted to personally check in.
> Would this week or next week make more sense to come back in?

## What Happens When a Patient Replies

Once a patient replies, the system responds immediately on the same channel.

### What Happens When a Patient Says "No" or "Not Right Now"

In recall, a "no" is rarely a rejection. It usually means not right now.

When a patient responds with:

- "Not right now"
- "Maybe later"
- "I'm busy"
- Any variation of deferring

the system does not push, reframe, or escalate authority.

Instead, the following happens:

- The response is acknowledged on the same channel
- No pressure or follow-up questions are introduced
- The patient exits the 72-hour sequence immediately
- No additional messages are sent during this window
- The patient is placed into a deferred follow-up pool for future outreach

This preserves trust, reduces opt-outs, and keeps the door open for re-engagement later.

**Important:** Silence is not treated as a "no." Only an explicit response pauses the sequence.

It proposes times, books the appointment, and updates the schedule automatically without staff involvement.

## Where Most Practices Break This

Even when a practice has "some kind of recall system," these are the exact failure points that kill results:

- Only one message goes out. No structured follow-up.
- Staff rewrite the messages. Copy loses authority and urgency.
- Responses after hours are missed. By morning, the window is gone.
- "Not now" replies get pushed. Which triggers resistance or unsubscribes.
- Sequences are paused "just in case." Which resets the whole loop.

Most dentists never see these breakdowns -- they just see empty chairs and assume "patients didn't want to come in." In reality, the system failed before the patient ever made a decision.

# Template Bank -- Full Recall Copy Library

This section contains the complete, ready-to-use recall copy library.

Templates are organized by:
- Voice (based on how overdue the patient is)
- Day in the 72-hour sequence (Day 0, Day 1, Day 3)

Each template is written to sound human, remove friction, and make responding easy.
Practices should use these templates exactly as written to maintain consistency and results.
