# Source Doc 17: Reviews + Referrals Breakdown

## The Problem

Most dental practices get 2-3 Google reviews per month despite seeing hundreds of patients. The gap between satisfied patients and published reviews is massive, and it's costing them visibility, rankings, and new patient acquisition.

## Key Stats

- 91% of patients would refer someone to their practice if asked. Only 11% of businesses ask.
- Google reviews are the #1 factor in local search ranking for dental practices.
- A practice with 300+ reviews and a 4.8 rating will outrank a practice with 47 reviews and a 5.0 rating.
- Review velocity (how many reviews per month) matters more than total count. Google rewards consistency.
- 72% of patients trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations.
- The average dental practice has a 3-6 month gap between their last burst of reviews and today.

## Why Manual "Ask for Reviews" Fails

1. Front desk is busy. They forget.
2. Patients say "sure" and then never do it. No follow-up.
3. No system for timing the ask. The best moment is 1-2 hours after the visit, not at checkout.
4. No routing logic. Happy patients should go to Google. Unhappy patients should go to private feedback.
5. No way to track who was asked and who wasn't.

## The Automated Review Collection System

### Flow:
1. Patient visits.
2. 1-2 hours after visit, automated text fires: "Hi [Name], thanks for coming in today! How was your experience? Reply 1-5."
3. Patient replies with a number.
4. Score 4-5: "Thanks! Would you mind sharing that on Google? Here's the link: [direct Google review link]"
5. Score 1-3: "Thanks for letting us know. We want to make this right. [Practice manager name] will reach out to you today."

### Why This Works:
- Timing: the patient still remembers the visit and is in a good mood.
- Frictionless: one-tap reply, direct link.
- Routing: happy patients become public advocates, unhappy patients get private recovery before it becomes a public complaint.
- Automated: no front desk involvement required.

## The Referral Pipeline

Most practices have zero referral system beyond "tell your friends about us."

### Structured referral approach:
1. After a positive review response (score 4-5), automated follow-up 24-48 hours later: "Glad you had a great experience! If you know anyone looking for a dentist, we'd love to take care of them too."
2. Referral tracking: who referred whom, conversion rate, lifetime value of referred patients.
3. Referred patients convert at 2-3x the rate of cold leads and stay 30% longer.

## Google Business Profile Impact

- Review count and velocity directly impact local pack ranking (the 3-pack that shows up in "dentist near me" searches).
- A practice that goes from 3 reviews/month to 15-20 reviews/month will see measurable ranking improvement within 60-90 days.
- Review responses from the practice (replying to reviews) also signal activity to Google.
- Photos, posts, and Q&A on the profile compound the effect.
