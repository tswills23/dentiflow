# LinkedIn Post Types -- Structural Definitions

8 post types rotated across the content calendar. Each has specific structural requirements that must be met for the post to qualify as that type.

---

## 1. Problem Awareness

**Purpose:** Show the pain practice owners are ignoring. Make invisible revenue leakage visible.

**Length:** 150-300 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must quantify the problem with real math (dollar amounts, percentages, patient counts)
- Must show the gap between what they think is happening and what's actually happening
- Must NOT offer a solution. The point is the problem itself.

**Hook style:** Surprising number or counterintuitive cost calculation

**Source docs:** #3 (Speed-to-Lead), #5 (Revenue Capture), #12 (72-Hour Playbook revenue math)

**Example angle:** "3 empty hygiene chairs per day at $200/chair = $156,000/year in lost production. Most practices don't track this number."

---

## 2. Framework / Education

**Purpose:** Give away full systems. Teach how things work at a level of detail that's actually useful.

**Length:** 300-500 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must present a complete, named framework or system (not just tips)
- Must have clear steps, stages, or components the reader can follow
- Must be detailed enough that someone could implement it

**Hook style:** Name the framework directly or lead with the outcome it produces

**Source docs:** #3 (Speed-to-Lead), #4 (Recall/Reactivation), #11 (Revenue OS), #12 (72-Hour Playbook)

**Example angle:** "The 72-Hour Recall Protocol has 3 rules. Break any of them and results collapse."

---

## 3. Contrarian / Pattern Interrupt

**Purpose:** Challenge what practice owners think they know. Create cognitive dissonance that makes them reconsider.

**Length:** 150-300 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must name a specific conventional belief and argue against it
- Must provide evidence, logic, or data for why the conventional belief is wrong
- Must NOT be contrarian for shock value. The alternative must be genuinely better.

**Hook style:** Direct contradiction of a widely-held belief

**Source docs:** #3 (Speed-to-Lead "staff blames lead quality"), #5 (Revenue Capture "they'll call when ready"), #13 (Money Model "free leads have no intention of buying = MYTH")

**Example angle:** "Your front desk is not the problem. Your response time is."

---

## 4. Full Playbook Giveaway

**Purpose:** Cornerstone long-form posts that give away entire protocols. The reader should be able to implement what you describe.

**Length:** 400-800 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must be a complete, step-by-step protocol or system
- Must include specific scripts, templates, or workflows where applicable
- Must cover failure modes or common mistakes
- This is the "if this is free, what's the paid version?" post type

**Hook style:** Bold promise or specific outcome ("Here's the complete recall system I use to recover $15K-$40K/month in lost hygiene revenue")

**Source docs:** #12 (72-Hour Playbook -- primary source), #14 (SMS Test Plan), #2 (30-Day Sprint)

**Example angle:** Full 72-Hour Recall Protocol with the 3 rules, day-by-day breakdown, and reply handling scripts.

---

## 5. Story / Credibility

**Purpose:** Build trust through lived experience. Connect Acquisition.com sales background to dental automation.

**Length:** 200-400 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must tell a specific story with concrete details (not vague "I learned...")
- Must connect the story to a lesson that applies to dental practice operations
- Must NOT be self-congratulatory. The story serves the lesson, not the ego.

**Hook style:** Story opener that creates a loop ("I sold $7M in 10 months. The skill that made that possible has nothing to do with sales.")

**Source docs:** #15 (AI Consultant System Prompt -- background), #13 (Money Model -- Hormozi frameworks applied)

**Example angle:** Selling at Acquisition.com taught me that speed beats quality in first contact. That's why our recall system responds in under 60 seconds.

---

## 6. Build in Public

**Purpose:** Share live test results, A/B data, what I'm building in real time. Radical transparency.

**Length:** 150-300 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must contain real data or test results (not hypothetical)
- Must show what worked AND what didn't
- Must be specific: numbers, dates, sample sizes, outcomes

**Hook style:** Lead with the result or the surprising finding

**Source docs:** #14 (SMS Test Plan -- A/B structure), #1 (MVP Agent SOPs -- system architecture)

**Example angle:** "We tested reason-based vs empathy-based openers on 500 overdue patients. Here's what happened."

---

## 7. Actionable Takeaway

**Purpose:** Give practice owners something they can use today. Scripts, templates, workflows, checklists.

**Length:** 200-400 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must include at least one copy-paste-ready script, template, or workflow
- Must be immediately usable without needing to buy anything or hire anyone
- Must specify context: when to use it, who says it, what response to expect

**Hook style:** Direct utility ("Here's the exact script your front desk should use when a patient calls about cost")

**Source docs:** #12 (72-Hour Playbook templates), #14 (SMS Test Plan reply handling), #3 (Speed-to-Lead recovery scripts)

**Example angle:** The 5 reply scripts that handle every recall patient response: yes, not now, cost question, who is this, and stop.

---

## 8. Myth Busting

**Purpose:** Name a specific myth practice owners believe, then dismantle it with data or logic.

**Length:** 150-300 words

**Structural requirements:**
- Must name the myth explicitly in quotes or as a direct statement
- Must provide specific evidence for why it's wrong (not just "I disagree")
- Must offer the correct alternative

**Hook style:** State the myth as if it's true, then reveal why it's wrong

**Source docs:** #4 (Recall "dirty lists"), #3 (Speed-to-Lead "leads aren't interested"), #13 (Money Model "free leads won't buy")

**Example angle:** "'Our recall list is cold.' No. Your recall list is uncontacted. There's a difference."
